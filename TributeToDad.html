Mr. Kenneth Lesher

04/06/1939 - 04/25/2020

Some information about Dad's life:

Dad was born April 6, 1939 in Brecknock Township, Lancaster County, PA. He was the youngest of 4 boys. He grew up on a farm in the country and went to a one room schoolhouse from kindergarten through 8th grade. Dad transferred to Governor Mifflin High School in Shillington to complete his high school education. What a shock that must have been, going from a one room school to a large high school. Dad played baseball in high school.

After high school dad worked at Bollman's Hat Factory and then at Becker's Garage. He eventually bought the garage from Harry Becker, renamed it Lesher's Garage, and ran the business until he retired.

He enjoyed playing softball, fishing, hunting and spending time with his family, especially his 2 grandsons.

Dad was 81 when he passed away from congestive heart failure and is missed dearly!

Dad's Obituary

Kenneth J. Lesher, 81, of Denver, passed away Sat., April 25, 2020 at Hospice & Community Care, Mount Joy. He was a son of the late Roy R. & Florence F. (Fritz) Lesher and the loving husband of 57 years to Nancy (Zell) Lesher.

Ken was a graduate of Gov. Mifflin H.S., class of 1958. He was a lifelong mechanic, owner of Lesher’s Garage in Adamstown until he retired in 2001. He played baseball and softball in local leagues and coached the Zinn’s Diner 35+ team. Ken enjoyed small game and deer hunting; he was a member of the Sunflower Hunting Camp in Lycoming County. An avid fisherman, he enjoyed trips to Delaware, fishing on the bay. A dedicated member of Peace UCC, he served as an Elder, a Deacon, and on the Muddy Creek Cemetery Board. He was a social member of the Adamstown VFW & Reamstown Athletic Assoc.

In addition to his wife, Ken is survived by two daughters, Beverly L. Madara (Michael G.) of Reinholds & Cynthia S. Anthony (Matthew P.) of Denver; two grandsons, James M. Anthony; Mark A. Anthony; and a brother, Frederick S. Lesher. He was predeceased by two brothers, Ivan W. Weitzel, Donald R. Lesher; & three sisters in-law, Nancy (wife of Fred), Helen (wife of Ivan), Arlene (wife of Donald).

Due to the current public health circumstances, the interment service is private in Muddy Creek Cemetery, Denver. A memorial service will be scheduled for a later date. In lieu of flowers, contributions to Peace UCC, 37 E. Swartzville Rd., Denver, Pa 17517.
